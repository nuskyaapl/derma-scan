DermaScan AI

AI-Powered Skin Lesion Analysis and Skin Cancer Detection System

Privacy Policy & Disclaimer

This page outlines how Dermascan AI handles user data and defines the limitations of the system. By using this application, you agree to the terms described below.

1. Purpose of the System

Dermascan AI is an Artificial Intelligence-based prototype developed as part of a Final Year Project. The system is designed to demonstrate the use of Deep Learning techniques in skin lesion classification, identifying images as benign or malignant.

This application is intended strictly for research, educational, and demonstration purposes.

2. Information We Collect

The system allows users to upload skin lesion images for analysis. The following data may be processed:

  • Uploaded image files
  • Prediction results generated by the system

No personal identification information (such as name, phone number, or address) is required to use the system.

3. Use of Data

All uploaded images are used solely for the purpose of generating predictions and analysis results. The system does not use this data for any other purpose such as training, sharing, or storage.

4. Data Storage and Privacy

Dermascan AI does not permanently store uploaded images or user data. All data is processed temporarily during the session and is not retained after processing.

Users are advised not to upload sensitive or personally identifiable images.

5. Third-Party Services

This system may use third-party services such as hosting providers and cloud-based tools. These services may have their own privacy policies and data handling practices.

6. Medical Disclaimer

⚠️ This system is not a medical diagnostic tool.

Dermascan AI is not a certified medical device and must not be used as a substitute for professional medical diagnosis, treatment, or advice.

The predictions generated by this system are based on a machine learning model and may not always be accurate. Users must consult a qualified healthcare professional for any medical concerns.

7. Accuracy and Limitations

Although the system is trained using publicly available datasets such as ISIC and HAM10000, it may produce incorrect results due to various factors, including:

  • Poor image quality
  • Improper lighting conditions
  • Non-skin or unsupported images
  • Variations not present in training data

Therefore, results should be interpreted with caution and must not be relied upon for clinical decisions.
